_"If you don't get rich now you'll be screwed in a few years."_

_"Make sure to collect your bag while you still can."_

It's ridiculous how many times I've heard this take come up unironically in San Francisco. From VCs, SWEs, to even the occasional Uber driver, it seems this theory has somehow wormed itself into every brain in the Valley.

> For those unfamiliar, the philosophy is: _Economic mobility will disappear as a result of AI automating jobs across all industries, freezing you in your socio-economic ranks at the time of collapse._

While I understand the concern itself, the part that baffles me is that people think making it into the upper class before the Armageddon somehow means you're safe. I made a short game called [Permanent Upper Crow](https://the-permanent-upper-crow.vercel.app/) to show the futility of it all.

A world where a majority of the population is suffering is not a world good for anyone. Even from a selfish perspective, does you making it into the elite class mean the people you care about will too? Who wants to eat caviar alone on the moon?

Dollars will only protect you from those that still respect it. Be bullish on humanity before yourself.
