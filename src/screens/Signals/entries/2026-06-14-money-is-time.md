_"Time is money."_ — Benjamin Franklin, 1748

We hear this wisdom so often, yet there is no interesting UI that reflects this in a practical way.

When I was in middle school, I was amazed by the trailer for [In Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In_Time). For those unfamiliar with Amanda Seyfried, In Time is a sci-fi film where time is currency in the most literal sense. Everyone has a subdermal tattoo with a ticking counter that, when fully elapsed, means death. All payments are pay-by-wrist, and everyone wants as much time as they can get.

> I don't quite remember how loans or venture capital work in this movie, but I would assume Blue Bottle takes pay-by-wrist.

I've thought about this film a lot more this year, as AI psychosis has made the relationship between time and business outcomes a conversation topic more common than the weather.

However, I find people more interesting than businesses a lot of the time, and thought it would be intriguing to view this from a more personal framing as well.

What if prices were represented in time instead of dollars? Would it make you feel differently about:

- Your spending?

- The wealth of others?

- Your current financial situation?

I made a simple site to visualize prices from this lens. [Money Is Time](https://money-is-time.jasonwu.ink/) allows you to enter your annual salary and visualize common purchases as a measure of your hard-working hours. It uses prices in San Francisco but if you want to fork it and localize it to your region it should take one or two prompts to do so.

Use your time wisely!
